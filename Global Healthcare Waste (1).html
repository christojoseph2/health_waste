1 Introduction

This report analyzes health care waste management services across various nations. By integrating UNICEF performance indicators we explore correlations between infrastructure, economic status, and health outcomes.

# Load UNICEF datasets using Polars
waste_pl = pl.read_csv("unicef_1_health care waste management service.csv")
meta_pl = pl.read_csv("unicef_metadata_filtered 3.csv")
gap_pl = pl.from_pandas(gapminder)

waste_latest = (
    waste_pl
    .sort("time_period", descending=True)
    .unique(subset=["alpha_3_code"])
)
# Merge UNICEF datasets for scatter plot and map analysis
full_unicef = waste_latest.join(
    meta_pl,
    left_on=["alpha_3_code", "time_period"],

    how="inner"
)
world_url = "https://raw.githubusercontent.com/nvkelso/natural-earth-vector/master/geojson/ne_110m_admin_0_countries.geojson"

2 Conclusion

This analysis highlights a significant disparity in health care infrastructure globally. The data reveals a clear inverse correlation between economic prosperity (GDP per Capita) and the lack of basic waste management services. While the Gapminder trends show a global rise in life expectancy, the UNICEF “No Service” indicators remind us that frontline health safety remains a critical challenge in low-income regions. Addressing these infrastructure gaps is essential for preventing secondary infections and ensuring environmental safety in healthcare delivery.
